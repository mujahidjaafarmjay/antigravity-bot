01Overview

The Antigravity SMC Bot is an automated crypto spot trading bot that runs 24/7 on Render's free cloud tier. It scans 25 Sharia-compliant pairs every 5 minutes, identifies high-probability Smart Money Concept (SMC) setups, and places trades on your Bybit account automatically.

All trade history is saved to Google Sheets so it survives Render restarts. You control the bot entirely from your phone via Telegram buttons — no coding required after setup.

2

06Trading Modes

The bot has three operating modes. Always start with paper and progress in order.

Mode comparison
📋
📋 PAPER
Uses real live prices from Bybit but places zero real orders. All logic runs normally — signals fire, SL/TP triggers, Telegram alerts send — but no money moves. The safest way to learn how the bot behaves. Start here. Run for at least 2 weeks.
🧪
🧪 TESTNET
Places real orders on Bybit Testnet using fake money. Requires a separate Bybit Testnet account and API key. Useful for testing order execution flow and Telegram alerts. Testnet prices are separate from real markets and behave differently.
🔴
🔴 LIVE
Places real orders with real money on your live Bybit account. Only switch to this mode after you have run paper mode for at least 2 weeks, reviewed all trade history in Google Sheets, and are satisfied with the signal quality.
Progression rule: Paper → Testnet → Live. Never skip steps. The daily loss limit (5%) and 2% per trade risk rule apply in all modes.

1

Free account — no credit card needed. Supports up to 50 monitors on the free plan.
2
Add a new monitor
Click Add New Monitor → Monitor Type: HTTP(s) → Friendly Name: Antigravity Bot → URL: https://YOUR-SERVICE-NAME.onrender.com/ping
3
Set interval to 5 minutes
Monitoring Interval: 5 minutes → Create Monitor. UptimeRobot will now ping /ping every 5 minutes. Your bot responds with OK and stays awake.
Once UptimeRobot is set up, visit https://YOUR-BOT.onrender.com/status in your browser to see a live JSON status of the bot including mode, active trades, and uptime.

10Telegram Commands

All bot control happens from Telegram. When you open your bot, you will see a keyboard with buttons at the bottom of the screen. Tap any button to send a command.

📊 Status
Shows current bot status (active/paused), USDT balance, trading mode, and number of open trades.
💰 Balance
Fetches your live USDT balance directly from Bybit API. Useful for confirming the bot can see your account.
📈 Open Trades
Lists all currently open positions with entry price, current price, stop loss, take profit, and unrealised P&L percentage.
📅 Profit Report
Reads your trade log and shows total trades, number closed, win rate, and total P&L in USDT.
🔍 Scan Setups
Manually triggers an immediate full scan of all 25 pairs. Useful when you think market conditions have changed and want to check right now.
🛑 Pause Bot
Stops the bot from opening any new trades. Existing open trades are still monitored and will close at SL/TP. Use this before news events or when you want manual control.
🚀 Resume Bot
Resumes scanning for new setups after pausing. Pause state persists through Render restarts via Google Sheets — resuming also saves to Sheets.
❓ Help
Shows a summary of all available commands and what each one does.
Pause before major news. High-impact events (Fed decisions, CPI, major crypto news) can cause violent price moves that trigger false signals. Tap 🛑 Pause Bot before such events and 🚀 Resume Bot after the market stabilises.

🛡️

The trade moved 1.5%+ in profit so the stop loss was automatically moved up to the entry price. The trade is now risk-free — worst case is a zero loss. No action needed.
✅

Trade closed at the take profit level. Shows exit price and profit percentage. No action needed. Trade is logged to Google Sheets and trades.csv automatically.
🛑

Trade closed at the stop loss. Loss is within the 2% risk rule. No action needed — losses are a normal part of trading. Review the trade in Google Sheets to learn from it.

The bot lost 5% of your balance in one day. Trading is automatically halted for the rest of the day. The bot resumes scanning the next day. If you want to resume early, tap 🚀 Resume Bot (use with caution).

13How the Strategy Works

The bot uses a Triple Screen analysis — three timeframes must align before any trade is taken. This reduces false signals significantly.

Screen 1 — Daily Trend Filter
Price must be above the 200-day moving average on the daily chart. If the macro trend is bearish, no buys are taken regardless of shorter timeframe signals.
Screen 2 — 4H Trend Confirmation
The 50-period MA must be above the 200-period MA on the 4H chart (Golden Cross structure). Both MAs need at least 200 candles of history — if data is insufficient, the signal is skipped.
Screen 3 — 1H Entry Signals
Three conditions must all be true on the 1H chart: (1) price is at or near a Bullish Order Block — the last bearish candle before an impulsive bullish move, (2) a Fair Value Gap exists nearby confirming imbalance, (3) volume is at least 1.2× the 20-candle average.
Entry Execution
A market buy order is placed. Stop loss is set at the lowest wick of the last 10 closed candles. Take profit is set at 2× the risk distance (1:2 reward-to-risk minimum).
Break-Even Shield
Once the trade moves 1.5% in profit, the stop loss is automatically moved to the entry price. The trade becomes risk-free — the worst outcome is breaking even.
Exit
Automatic exit at stop loss (loss capped at 2% of capital) or take profit (minimum 4% gain). All exits are logged to Google Sheets and trades.csv.
Important detail: All signals are evaluated on the last closed candle only — never the live, still-forming candle. This prevents false signals from incomplete candle data.

15Sharia Compliance Filter

Every pair is checked against the whitelist in config.py before any trade is placed. The bot will never trade a pair not on this list, regardless of any signal.

The 25 default pairs were selected based on the Islamic Finance screening criteria from Chapter 5 of your reference document: each must qualify as Māl (genuine utility, retrievable, desirable), must have Taqawwum (lawful use case), and must not be primarily associated with riba-based finance, gambling, or haram industries.

Adding or removing pairs

Open config.py and edit the WHITELIST_PAIRS list. Before adding any new coin:

1
Research the coin's utility
What does it actually do? Does it have genuine real-world utility beyond price speculation? Does it qualify as Māl under the criteria in your reference document?
2
Check the business activity
Is the project's core activity Sharia-compliant? Lending/borrowing protocols (like AAVE, Compound) are questionable due to riba. Gambling or gaming tokens are prohibited.
3
Consult a qualified scholar if unsure
For significant amounts, consult a qualified Islamic finance scholar before trading a new coin. The bot enforces whatever is on your list — the screening responsibility is yours.
16Recommended Progression Plan
W1
Week 1–2 — Paper mode
Run in paper mode. Read every single Telegram signal. After each potential setup or trade entry alert, open Bybit and look at that chart yourself. Ask: does this signal make sense? Is it near a real support zone? Does it match your SMC knowledge? Build familiarity with how the bot thinks.
W3
Week 3–4 — Review paper results
Open your Google Sheet. Review every trade taken in paper mode. Calculate the actual win rate, average profit, average loss. If the results look reasonable (40%+ win rate, positive overall P&L) — consider moving to testnet. If not, stay in paper and investigate why signals are wrong.
M2
Month 2 — Testnet
Switch MODE to testnet in Render env vars. Create a Bybit Testnet account at testnet.bybit.com and generate separate API keys. Run for 2 more weeks. Verify that order placement, SL/TP execution, and Telegram alerts all work correctly end-to-end.
M3
Month 3 — Live with small capital
Switch MODE to live. Start with your current $40–$50. The 2% risk rule means each trade risks $0.80–$1.00. This is the right size to learn on. Do not add more capital until you have 30+ live trades logged and are satisfied with the results.
M4+
Month 4+ — Scale gradually
Only increase capital after consistent profitability. The bot's risk rules scale automatically with your balance — 2% of $200 is $4 per trade, 2% of $1000 is $20 per trade. Never deposit more than you can afford to lose entirely.
17Troubleshooting
Bot deployed but no Telegram message received

Check: (1) Is TELEGRAM_TOKEN correct in Render env vars? (2) Did you tap Start on your bot in Telegram? (3) Is TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID your correct numeric ID? Check Render logs for [Telegram] send error: lines.

Balance shows $0 in Status

The API key cannot read your balance. Check: (1) API key has Read permission enabled in Bybit. (2) You copied the correct key and secret — no extra spaces. (3) If on testnet, are you using testnet API keys with MODE=testnet? Live keys do not work on testnet and vice versa.

Bot stops scanning after a few hours

UptimeRobot is not configured or the ping URL is wrong. Check that UptimeRobot is actively monitoring https://YOUR-BOT.onrender.com/ping with a 5-minute interval and showing as UP (green). Also check the Render logs for any crash errors.

Google Sheets not connecting

Check: (1) The entire JSON content of the service account key is pasted as GOOGLE_SHEETS_CREDENTIALS — no truncation. (2) The sheet name exactly matches GOOGLE_SHEET_NAME. (3) The sheet is shared with the service account email as Editor. Render logs will show [Sheets] Connection failed: with the specific error.

Profit Report says "No trade history yet"

The bot has not placed any trades yet (normal in early paper mode), or you are in paper mode and no signals have fired. Alternatively, check Render logs for TradeLogger errors. The trade log file is at logs/trades.csv inside the container.

Daily loss limit triggered unexpectedly

The 5% daily loss limit resets at midnight UTC. If it triggered, the bot will automatically resume the next day. To resume manually before midnight, tap 🚀 Resume Bot in Telegram — but only do this if you have investigated why the losses occurred.

18Security Rules
These rules are non-negotiable. Violating any of them can result in loss of your entire Bybit balance.
🔒
Never enable withdrawal permissions on your API key
Your Bybit API key needs only Read and Spot Trade. Never tick the withdrawal box. Even if someone steals your key, they cannot withdraw funds without withdrawal permission enabled.
🔒
Never commit secrets to GitHub
The .gitignore excludes .env files. But always double-check config.py before every push. All values should be os.environ.get(...) — never actual key strings.
🔒
Set IP restriction on your Bybit API key
In Bybit → API Management → your key → set IP restriction to your Render service's outbound IPs. Render outbound IPs are listed in your service's settings. This prevents the key working from any other IP.
🔒
Keep your GitHub repo private
Even without hardcoded secrets, a public repo exposes your strategy logic, pair whitelist, and risk settings. Keep the repo private at all times.
🔒
Rotate API keys periodically
Every 3 months, generate new Bybit API keys, update them in Render env vars, and delete the old ones in Bybit. Update your Telegram bot token if you suspect it was compromised.
